Project Overview

Identifying Risk Drivers to Maximize Retention

A leading telecommunications provider faced rising customer attrition, with churn reaching 26.5%. Their generic retention strategy was ineffective, often targeting low-risk customers while missing those most likely to leave.

I developed a machine learning pipeline to analyze over 7,000 customer profiles and pinpoint behavioral and contractual drivers of churn. This enabled a shift from reactive problem-solving to a proactive, data-driven retention strategy.

Business Impact

The engine analyzes 21 customer features, including tenure, contract type, and service usage, to generate churn risk scores. By optimizing for recall rather than overall accuracy, the model identifies nearly 70% of potential churners, enabling the marketing team to deliver targeted loyalty offers before cancellations occur.

Problem Statement

The Challenge

The existing retention process faced key challenges that were directly affecting the company‚Äôs revenue and profitability:

  • High Acquisition Costs: Acquiring a new customer costs 5x more than retaining an existing one, draining revenue unnecessarily.
  • Contractual Vulnerability: 70% of churners were on Month-to-Month contracts, but no system flagged them for timely retention actions.
  • Service Dissatisfaction: Fiber Optic customers were leaving at higher rates, yet the underlying issues remained unquantified.
  • Data Imbalance: With only ~26% of customers churning, standard models struggled to detect risk without bias, limiting predictive accuracy.
Methodology

Solution Approach

Phase 1: Discovery
Discovery & Data Cleaning
Aggregated 7,043 customer records, handled missing TotalCharges values, and performed exploratory data analysis to confirm a 26.5% churn imbalance, driving the use of a stratified split and a weighted modeling approach.
Phase 2: Feature Engineering
Feature Engineering & Encoding
Developed a hybrid encoding logic:
  • Binary Mapping: Converted Yes/No categorical variables to 0/1 for model compatibility.
  • Normalization: Standardized "No Internet/Phone Service" to 0 to ensure consistent feature representation.
  • One-Hot Encoding: Applied One-Hot Encoding to InternetService, Contract, and PaymentMethod.
Phase 3: Model Development
Model Development & Benchmarking
Developed an XGBoost model using scale_pos_weight to address class imbalance, prioritizing detection of churners (the minority class) while tolerating some false positives for maximum retention impact.
Phase 4: Validation
Validation & Optimization
Performed an 80/20 stratified split and evaluated model performance using confusion matrices and classification reports, optimizing F1-score to balance precision and recall for maximum business value.
Phase 5: Insights
Actionable Insights
Extracted feature importance to create a ‚ÄòChurn Driver‚Äô dashboard and generated actionable insights, enabling targeted retention campaigns based on the highest-risk factors.
Technical Implementation

Technical Deep Dive

Implemented a Gradient Boosted Tree architecture, specifically optimized to handle imbalanced classification and accurately identify high-risk churners.

Insights & Reflections

Key Learnings

This project showcased the power of predictive modeling in proactive customer relationship management. Beyond basic classification, it demonstrated how data-driven insights can directly safeguard revenue and inform strategic decisions. Key takeaways include:

1. Recall > Accuracy for Business Value

In churn prediction, missing an at-risk customer (False Negative) is far costlier than offering a discount to a loyal one (False Positive). XGBoost proved superior by achieving higher recall and capturing more churners.

Lesson:Align evaluation metrics with the business‚Äôs cost-benefit priorities to maximize impact.

2. Contract Structure is Strongest Predictor

Analysis revealed a stark contrast in contract types: month-to-month customers were far more likely to churn than those on one- or two-year contracts. Longer-term commitments act as a natural buffer against churn.

Lesson: Leverage ‚Äòbehavioral anchors‚Äô in your data that inherently drive customer loyalty.

3. The "Value Paradox" of High Charges

High monthly charges alone weren‚Äôt the main churn driver; the combination of high charges and short tenure proved most critical. Customers who hadn‚Äôt yet realized full service value were the most price-sensitive.

Lesson: The first 12 months of a customer‚Äôs lifecycle are key for reinforcing value and building long-term loyalty.

4. Explainability Drives Action

Stakeholders were hesitant to invest in retention campaigns based on a ‚Äòblack box‚Äô model. Leveraging SHAP values to reveal that fiber-optic service issues and insufficient tech support were driving churn transformed the model into a clear roadmap for service improvements.

Lesson: Stakeholders need not just predictions of who will churn, but actionable insights on what to change to retain them.

Final Summary

Conclusion

This project successfully demonstrates how machine learning shifts customer retention from reactive damage control to proactive revenue protection. By identifying churn drivers like contract type and service quality, the company can proactively secure its most vulnerable revenue streams.

Key Recommendations

  • Focus retention campaigns on high-risk customers, including those on month-to-month contracts, with short tenure, or high charges.

  • Promote TechSupport and OnlineSecurity services to enhance perceived value and loyalty.

  • Improve service quality for high-value customer segments to reduce churn risk.

  • Continuously monitor campaign outcomes and retrain models quarterly to maintain predictive accuracy.

Impact

By using predictive analytics to proactively address churn, companies can increase customer loyalty, reduce revenue loss, and optimize marketing investments. High-risk customers can be identified early, allowing for targeted interventions that protect critical revenue streams.

Predictive insights also reveal key drivers of churn such as contract type, tenure, and service quality. Companies can use this information to tailor campaigns, improve services, and continuously refine strategies to strengthen long-term customer relationships.
